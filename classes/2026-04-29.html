1. Study Text: “The San Lucas Clinic Expansion”

Read the text below and pay attention to the bold verbs.

“As an architect, I have worked on many healthcare projects since 2010. Last year, my team designed the new wing of the San Lucas Clinic. We finished the blueprints in June, and the construction started right after.

However, we have faced some challenges recently regarding the HVAC system. This morning, I inspected the consultation rooms and found a problem with the air exchange rate. I have already spoken to the engineers, but they haven’t fixed the issue yet.

In my career, I have seen many power outages, but I never saw anything like what happened yesterday. The backup generator failed during a medical imaging test. We have already updated the technical report, and the stakeholders requested a meeting for tomorrow.”

2. Prática de Aula (Grammar & Drills)

Present Perfect & Nuances

  1. “They have just postponed the meeting about the new imaging room.” (Action in the very recent past).

  2. “Have you finished the workshop proposal for the board yet?” (Expectation).

  3. “We have collaborated with this hospital for over five years.” (Duration/Status).

Conditionals (Review)

  1. “If the room were sterile, the doctors would start the surgery now.”

  2. “If I were the manager, I would change the inclusion policy.”

Pronunciation & Vocabulary Notes

  • Proposal: /prəˈpəʊzl/ (pro-PÔU-zal).

  • Issue: /ˈɪʃuː/ (Í-chuu).

  • Policy (Política) vs. Police (Polícia).

  • Postponed: Adiado.

3. Reading Comprehension & Grammar Check

  1. Experience: How long has the architect worked on healthcare projects? > Since 2010.

  2. Specific Event: When did the team finish the blueprints for the San Lucas Clinic? > In June.

  3. Current Status: Why does the architect use the Present Perfect to talk about the engineers (“I have already spoken…”)? > Because it connects a past action to the current situation/status.

  4. Problem Solving: What happened yesterday during the medical imaging test? > The backup generator failed.

  5. Unfinished vs. Finished: Why switch from Present Perfect to Past Simple in the last paragraph? > Switch to Past Simple to describe the specific event that “happened yesterday”.
