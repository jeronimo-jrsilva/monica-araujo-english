Objetivos Realizados

  3. Phonetic Contrasts: Diferenciação entre Whole (/hoʊl/) e Hole (/hoʊl/) - Homófonos.

New Case Study: The India Residence Project

Análise do briefing técnico para o novo desafio do escritório:

“Recently our office received an indication for a residence project, which will be built in a condominium in the city of Karmal in India.

Apparently it is a simple project, however it is a challenge, considering that we are not used to the local legislation for architectural projects and urban parameters, so we will have to study about it.

Also, we will have to present all the work in English, which is also a new challenge for our office, as we intend to expand horizons and sell residential projects through e-commerce.

We hope that this will be a project that will open doors for our production outside of Brazil.”

Grammar: Too vs. Enough

1. TOO + Adjective/Adverb (Excesso)

Usado para indicar que algo ultrapassa o limite aceitável.

  • “The glare in the reception is too strong. We need a filter.”

  • “This hallway is too dark for safety standards.”

  • “This is too good to be true.”

Advanced: Too Much vs. Too Many

  • Too much + Uncountable: “This is too much water.” / “There is too much trouble (/ˈtrʌbl/).”

  • Too many + Countable: “There are too many children in the room.”

2. Adjective/Adverb + ENOUGH (Suficiente)

O “Enough” vem DEPOIS do adjetivo.

  • “Is the light bright enough for the surgery?”

  • “The response time of the sensors wasn’t fast enough.”

3. ENOUGH + Noun (Quantidade Suficiente)

O “Enough” vem ANTES do substantivo.

  • “We don’t have enough fixtures for the whole corridor.”

  • “Do we have enough budget for this?”

Pronunciation & Homophones

  • Whole (/hoʊl/) - Todo/Inteiro/Totalidade.

  • Hole (/hoʊl/) - Buraco/Abertura.

    • Context: “We need a whole day to fix the hole in the wall.”

  • Enough (/ɪˈnʌf/) - Som de ‘F’ no final.

  • India (/ˈɪn.di.ə/) - Atenção à tônica no início.
