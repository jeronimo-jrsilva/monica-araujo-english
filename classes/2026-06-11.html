Topic: Operational Phrasal Verbs & Advanced Causative Scenarios

Mastering Phrasal Verbs for project management and the Causative Form for clinical delegation.

1. Phrasal Verbs for PMs

  • Carry out: Executar / Cumprir. (e.g., "Carry out an order"). Pronunciation: Carry vs Care.
  • Look into: Investigar / Analisar. (e.g., "Look into the discrepancy").
  • Call off: Cancelar. (e.g., "The board called off the meeting").
  • Hold up: Atrasar / Reter. (e.g., "The project was held up").
  • Iron out: Resolver pequenos detalhes. Pronunciation: Iron (/ˈaɪərn/).

2. Possessives Review

'S Structure: Jane's house, Company's budget, My uncle's boat.

3. Causative Form Crisis (Delegation)

Structure: HAVE + SOMETHING + DONE

  • Biohazard Leak: "I'll have the area isolated."
  • X-Ray Lead: "I'll have the shielding replaced."
  • ICU Air: "I'll have the filters checked."
  • Compliance: "I'll have the signs reprinted."

4. Vocabulary Notes

  • Subcontractor: Empreiteiro / Terceirizado.
  • Issue: Problema / Contratempo.
  • Loss: Perda / Prejuízo vs Lost: Perdido.
  • Patch: Consertar / Remendar (Patch panel / Patchwork).
