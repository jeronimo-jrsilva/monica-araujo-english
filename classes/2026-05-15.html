🎯 Resumo da Realização

Exploração da ciência milenar indiana Vastu Shastra e sua aplicação prática na arquitetura moderna. Foco na tradução de conceitos filosóficos para o inglês técnico (BIM/ArchiCAD) e análise de fluxos energéticos em projetos de alto padrão.

🏗️ 1. The Five Elements (Panchbhootas)

Vastu Shastra harmonizes living spaces with nature by mapping elements to cardinal directions:

  • Earth (Bhoomi): Linked to stability and foundations. Ideal for the South-West quadrant (Master Bedroom). It provides security and safety.
  • Water (Jala): Represents flow and purity. Ideal for the North-East direction (Meditation areas, pools).
  • Fire (Agni): Linked to energy and transformation. Best for the South-East (The Kitchen).
  • Air (Vayu): Movement and communication. Best for the North-West (Guest rooms, children's bedrooms).
  • Space (Akasha): Emptiness and circulation. The Center (Brahmsthan) of the house. A clear concept here favors circulation.

🗺️ 2. Zoning and Flow (Spatial Organization)

Key rules for healthy residential energy:

  • The Main Entrance (The "Mouth" of Energy): It should preferably face North or East to guarantee morning sunlight (Eastern sunlight), which is vitalizing and purifying.
  • The Kitchen (The Heart of the House): Placed in the South-East to align cooking functions with the fire element (Agni), promoting vitality.
  • The Master Bedroom: Placed in the South-West (Earth) to provide anchoring and stability for the providers of the family.
  • The Center (Brahmsthan): In high-end projects, the center should remain empty or act as an internal courtyard/atrium. It allows vital energy to circulate freely.
  • Circulation Rules: We must avoid doors in a straight line so the energy doesn't "run" too fast. Stairs (heavy structures) should go to the South or West (Western side), not suffocating the lighter zones.

🏢 3. Façade Design and Massing (Technical Specs)

Converting Vastu principles into professional design statements:

  • North and East Façades (Lightness): "These are auspicious directions. We design them with openness in mind, using large glazed areas (glazing), balconies, and slim frames/profiles."
  • South and West Façades (Protection): "These need thermal mass. We use fewer openings, shading devices (brise-soleil), thicker walls, or stone cladding to prevent overheating."
    Note: Cladding is for external surfaces; Coating is for internal surfaces.
  • Proportion and Rhythm: "We avoid extremely asymmetrical façades or sharp angles (poison arrows) that cut the energy."
  • Site and Topography: "The plinth (podium) must be higher than the street level. If the terrain is on sloping ground (downhill/uphill), we use the foundation to elevate the perception of the house."
