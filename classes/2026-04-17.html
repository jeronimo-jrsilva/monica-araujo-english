🏗️ Real Life Recap (PAS Gold Recovery)

Consolidando as atividades reais discutidas na sessão de hoje:

🕒 Yesterday (Past Simple)

  • "I spoke English, went to work and worked all day."
  • "I gave a presentation/speech about accessibility and inclusion (Workshop)."
  • "I was in this event all afternoon."
  • "I cooked dinner."

⚡ Now (Present Continuous)

  • "I am speaking English with my teacher online."
  • "I am drinking a super vitamin (papaya, sizzle...)."
  • Grammar Note: Become (tornar-se).

📅 Plan (Future: Going to)

  • "I'm going to rest this Sunday all day long."
  • "Maybe/perhaps/perchance/mayhaps I'm going to the club with my children."
  • "I'm going to church because I don't like to start a week without praying."
  • "I'm going to study and write about my dissertation on Saturday."
  • "Possibly I am going to drink wine because I deserve!"

🔄 Tense Consolidation: The Architect's Timeline

  • Past Simple: "We designed the floor plan last week." (Completed tasks)
  • Present Simple: "The hospital requires accessibility ramps." (Facts / Regulations)
  • Present Continuous: "The team is updating the CAD files now." (Current actions)
  • Future (Going to): "We are going to present the budget on Monday." (Planned steps)
  • Future (Will): "I will call the contractor to fix this." (Decisions/Predictions)

🗣️ Technical Vocabulary & Context

  • Accessibility and Inclusion: /ækˌsesə'bɪləti/ (Foco no painel de ontem).
  • Dissertation: /ˌdɪsər'teɪʃn/ (Trabalho acadêmico).
  • Vocabulary synonyms: Perhaps → Perchance → Mayhaps (para variar o 'Maybe').
  • Schedule / Deadline: "I will update the schedule after my dissertation."
