1. The Causative Form: Delegation Power

Na gestão de projetos e arquitetura, você raramente executa o trabalho braçal. Você delega. Quando alguém faz algo para você, usamos a estrutura:

HAVE + SOMETHING + DONE (V3)

Exemplo: "I'll have the HVAC repaired." (Eu vou mandar consertar o ar-condicionado).

Rapid-Fire Delegation Drills:

Cenário (Trigger) Ação Delegada (Reflex)
"The server room is overheating.""I'll have the AC fixed."
"The hospital signs are in Portuguese only.""I'll have them translated."
"The MRI machine is vibrating.""I'll have it inspected."
"The walls are dirty.""I'll have them repainted."
"The software is crashing.""I'll have it updated."
"The parking lot lights are off.""I'll have the bulbs replaced."
"The security protocol is old.""I'll have it revised."
"The floor is slippery.""I'll have it cleaned."
"The windows are broken.""I'll have them repaired."
"The inventory is low.""I'll have more supplies ordered."

2. Time Mastery: Past Simple vs. Present Perfect

A precisão no tempo define sua autoridade. Use Past Simple para eventos finalizados com data marcada e Present Perfect para experiências e histórico sem data específica.

Past Simple

Focado no QUANDO (Yesterday, 2 hours ago).

"I finished the report yesterday."

Present Perfect

Focado no STATUS ou EXPERIÊNCIA (Recently, Ever, Never).

"I have never worked with this material."

Precision Drill:

  • "Did you call the subcontractor this morning?" (Tempo específico: esta manhã).
  • "I have never worked with this material before." (Experiência de vida/carreira).
  • "We finished the foundation last month." (Tempo específico: mês passado).
