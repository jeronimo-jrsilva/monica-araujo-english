Class 38: Precision in Reporting & The Pre-Audit Challenge

📅 Lesson Info

  • Date: 2026-06-13
  • Topic: Precision in Reporting & The Pre-Audit Challenge

Consolidação de Relative Clauses e aplicação prática de Phrasal Verbs e Causative Form em um cenário de Auditoria Governamental.

🏗️ 1. Precision Review: Relative Clauses (Identity & Function)

Objective: Defining technical roles and documents with precision.

Prompt: “Define these items using WHO / WHICH / THAT.”

  1. A Subcontractor: “A person who repairs the HVAC in the ICU.”
  2. A Blueprint: “A document which shows the structural design.”
  3. A Bottleneck: “A situation that delays the project progress.”
  4. The Board: “The group who approves the budget.”
  5. A Punch List: “A list which contains the remaining small tasks.”
  6. A Stakeholder: “Anyone who is affected by the project.”

⚡ 2. Review Drill: The Causative Matrix

Quick-fire delegation exercise.

  • Scenario: The hospital elevator is stuck.
  • Action: “I’ll have it fixed.”
  • Scenario: The floor tiles are cracked.
  • Action: “I’ll have them replaced.”
  • Scenario: The budget was rejected.
  • Action: “I’ll have it revised.”

🏆 3. The Final Challenge: The Pre-Audit Briefing

Goal: Brief your team for the “Government Audition”.

The Context: The government audit starts in 2 hours. There are still issues with the X-Ray shielding and the biohazard signs.

Your Task: Create a 1-minute briefing using: 1. At least 2 Phrasal Verbs (Carry out, Look into, Iron out, etc.). 2. At least 2 Causative Forms (Have something done). 3. Contrast Connectors (Despite / Although).

Example: “Despite the delay, we will carry out the final inspection. I have already had the shielding checked…”

📚 4. Vocabulary Expansion: Management Terms

  • To Brief: Dar instruções/resumo.
  • To Oversee: Supervisionar.
  • Lead Time: Prazo de entrega / Tempo de espera.
