1. Grammar Drilling: Integrating Structure & Concept

Monica, trabalhamos a precisão técnica usando Voz Passiva e Orações Relativas:

  • Passive Voice + Modais:
    • “The main entrance should be placed in the North or East quadrant.”
    • “Negative energy can be blocked by using thick walls and stone cladding in the South.”
    • “When building on sloping ground, the plinth needs to be designed carefully to ensure anchoring and stability.”
  • Relative Clauses (who/which/that/where):
    • “The Brahmsthan is the area that/which is located at the center of the house and must remain empty.”
    • “Mônica is the architect who specializes in Healthcare Design and Vastu Shastra.”
    • “Of the two façades, the North one is the one which provides more light.”
    • “The poison arrows are the ones that we must avoid in the project.”

2. Phonetic Lab & Vocabulary

Pontos de atenção na pronúncia e vocabulário:

  • Place vs. To Place: Place (Lugar) / To Place (Colocar, posicionar).

  • Thick vs. Think: Thick (Espesso/Grosso - Som de ‘TH’ soprado) vs. Think (Pensar).

  • South: /saʊθ/ (Atenção ao som do ‘AU’).

  • Which vs. Witch: Homófonos. Which (Qual) vs. Witch (Bruxa).

  • Anchor: /‘æŋkər/ (Som de ’K’ forte).

  • Poison: /’pɔɪzn/ (Veneno).

3. Mônica’s Project Application

Exercício de conversão (Active to Passive):

  • Original: “The laundry space doesn’t exist in Vastu program.”

  • Application (Toilet Location):

    • Active: “The team located the toilet at the entrance of the house.”
    • Passive: “The toilet was located at the entrance of the house.”
    • Present Perfect Passive: “The toilet has been located at the entrance…”
