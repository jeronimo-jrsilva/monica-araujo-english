Class 07: Future Perspectives: Will vs. Going to (Project Deadlines & Decisions)

📅 Lesson Info

  • Date: 2026-04-16
  • Topic: Future Perspectives: Will vs. Going to (Project Deadlines & Decisions)

Objetivo

Diferenciar o uso de Will (decisões imediatas/previsões) e Going to (planos/intenções), aplicando o vocabulário de prazos, aprovações e cronogramas de arquitetura hospitalar.

1. Warm-up: Project Status

Review: What is your team doing this week to find new specialists?

  • (Check Homework sentences)

2. The Two Futures: Comparison

A. “Going to” (The Planned Future)

Usamos para planos já decididos, intenções claras ou evidências concretas. Structure: Subject + AM/IS/ARE + GOING TO + Verb

  • Example: “We are going to approve the floor plan this afternoon.” (A reunião já está marcada).

  • Example: “The client is going to visit the construction site tomorrow.” (Já foi agendado).

B. “Will” (The Spontaneous Future / Predictions)

Usamos para decisões tomadas no momento da fala, promessas ou previsões incertas. Structure: Subject + WILL + Verb

  • Example: “.” (Decisão imediata).

  • Example: “I think the project will be ready in six months.” (Previsão/Opinião).

3. Technical Vocabulary: Project Management

Verbos essenciais para alinhar o cronograma (schedule) e correções gramaticais:

Term (EN) Translation Real Class Examples
Schedule Agenda / Cronograma “My schedule this week is full.”
Postpone Adiar “I couldn’t postpone my visit to the doctor.”
Approve Aprovar “I don’t approve this behavior.”
Update Atualizar “Can you update me?”
Deadline Prazo final “The deadline is non-negotiable.”

Grammar Quick Fix

  • This (singular) -> These (plural)

  • That (singular) -> Those (plural)

4. Practice: Decision vs. Plan

Monica, como você diria estas situações?

  1. Plano: “Eu vou viajar para Goiás na próxima segunda.”
    • I am going to travel to Goiás next Monday.
  2. Decisão Imediata: “O cliente mudou de ideia? Ok, eu vou ligar para ele agora.”
    • The client changed his mind? Okay, I will call him now.
  3. Previsão: “Eu acho que esse hospital vai ficar pronto antes do prazo.”
    • I think this hospital will be ready before the deadline.

5. Contrast Table for Daily Use

Situation Use Example
Planned Meeting Going to We are going to meet at 3 PM.
Instant Offer Will I will help you with those files.
Intentions Going to I am going to study more technical terms.
Promises Will I will send the report by the end of the day.

6. Drills: Reading & Listening Practice

Part A: Reading Practice (Foco em Pronúncia e Fluidez)

Monica, leia em voz alta focando na entonação técnica:

  1. “We are going to reconfigure the emergency exit according to the new safety regulations.”

  2. “I believe the integration of AI in hospital design will reduce patient stress significantly.”

  3. “The engineering team is going to install the HVAC system by the end of next month.”

  4. “If the budget increases, we will expand the pediatric ward to include more beds.”

  5. “Next Monday, I am going to present the final blueprints to the hospital directors.”

Part B: Listening Practice (Foco em Compreensão e Velocidade)

Jeronimo lerá em velocidade natural. Tente identificar se é um plano (going to) ou uma decisão/promessa (will):

  1. “Don’t worry about the floor plan; I’ll update it right now.” (Will - Decisão imediata).

  2. “The hospital board is going to review our proposal on Wednesday morning.” (Going to - Plano).

  3. “I think the sustainable materials will make the building more energy-efficient.” (Will - Previsão).

  4. “We aren’t going to change the laboratory layout this week.” (Going to - Intenção negativa).

  5. “Wait! I’ll grab the measurements for the radiology room for you.” (Will - Oferta imediata).
