⏳ Will vs. Going to

Diferenciando o uso de decisões espontâneas e previsões (Will) de planos e intenções (Going to):

A. "Will" (Spontaneous / Predictions)

  • "The phone is ringing. I will answer it."
  • "I think the project will be ready in six months."
  • "I travel every year. I will travel next year."

B. "Going to" (Planned Future)

  • "The client is going to visit the construction site tomorrow."
  • "We are going to approve the floor plan this afternoon."

🏥 Technical Vocabulary: Project Management

  • Schedule: Agenda / Cronograma ("My schedule this week is full").
  • Postpone: Adiar ("I couldn't postpone my visit to the doctor").
  • Approve: Aprovar ("I don't approve this behavior").
  • Update: Atualizar ("Can you update me?").
  • Deadline: Prazo final ("The deadline is non-negotiable").

💡 Grammar Tip: This vs. These

  • This (Singular) → These (Plural)
  • That (Singular) → Those (Plural)

📖 Practice Drills

  • "We are going to reconfigure the emergency exit."
  • "I believe the integration of AI will reduce patient stress."
  • "The engineering team is going to install the HVAC system."
