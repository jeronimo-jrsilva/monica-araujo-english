Class 09: Comparing Environments: Comparatives & Superlatives in Healthcare Design

📅 Lesson Info

  • Date: 2026-04-20
  • Topic: Comparing Environments: Comparatives & Superlatives in Healthcare Design

Objetivo

Habilitar a aluna a analisar e comparar criticamente soluções arquitetônicas, materiais e fluxos urbanos/hospitalares utilizando as estruturas de comparativos e superlativos.

1. Review & Foundations: The Architecture of Comparison

Usamos comparativos para estabelecer relação entre dois elementos e superlativos para destacar o topo de uma categoria.

A. Comparatives (A vs B)

Structure: Noun 1 + (to be) + [Adjective + er / More + Adjective] + THAN + Noun 2

  • Short Form (+er):
    • “The left side building is bigger than the right side building.”
    • “I’m taller than the average woman.”
    • “I’m smaller than the reference for women.”
  • Long Form (More…):
    • “Argentinian wine is worse than Chilean wine.” (Irregular: Bad -> Worse).
    • “Steel is more resistant than plastic.”

2. Technical Vocabulary: Materials & Sustainability

Vocabulário expandido durante a aula para aplicações em Healthcare Design:

Glass Vidro “Glass is better than wood (for sterilization).”

3. Superlatives: The Best in Class

Structure: The + [Adjective + est / Most + Adjective]

  • Short Form:
    • “The tallest mountain is Everest.”
    • “What is the shortest way to Carrefour?” (Shortest path/route).
  • Irregulars & Technical Choices:
    • “Glass is better than wood, but steel is the best.”
    • “Turin is better than Napoli, but Rome is the best.”

4. Technical Drills & Phonetics

Monica, foco nestas variações de adjetivos curtos:

  1. Big -> Bigger -> The Biggest

  2. Small -> Smaller -> The Smallest

  3. Tall -> Taller -> The Tallest

  4. Cheap -> Cheaper -> The Cheapest

  5. Pretty -> Prettier -> The Prettiest
