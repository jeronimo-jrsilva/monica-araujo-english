📅 Lesson Info

  • Date: 2026-05-13

🎯 Resumo da Realização

Mastering Who, Which, That, Where to connect technical ideas and create fluid descriptions. Introduction to One/Ones as essential substitutes to avoid repetition in architectural and medical specifications.

⚡ Warm-up: Passive Voice Review

Quick check from Class 24 (The "Report" Tone):

  • "We delivered the project yesterday." -> The project was delivered yesterday.
  • "The technicians follow the safety standards." -> Safety standards are followed by the technicians.
  • "Mônica chose the sustainable material." -> The sustainable material was chosen by Mônica.

Safety vs. Security: Safety (Passive/Protection/Standards) vs. Security (Active/Vigilance/Systems).

🚀 Technical Grammar 1: Relative Clauses

Relative Clauses connect ideas and create fluid technical descriptions:

  • WHO (People): "The specialist who calculates the load-bearing capacity..."
  • WHICH (Things): "The standard which regulates hospital lighting..."
  • THAT (Essential Info): "The only material that meets the budget is vinyl."
  • WHERE (Places): "The area where the vaccines are stored must have backup power."

🚀 Technical Grammar 2: The Power of One / Ones

Used to substitute a noun already mentioned, avoiding monotony:

  • Identifying: "I prefer the ones with the open floor plan."
  • Contrasting: "These fixtures are good, but the ones we used in the previous project were better."
  • The "Special" One: "The one who leads the BIM coordination is Mônica."

🧠 Practical Drills (Classroom Results)

  • "We have a contractor. He is responsible for the foundations."
    -> The contractor WHO is responsible for the foundations.
  • "I am looking for the blueprint. It shows the plumbing system."
    -> I am looking for the blueprint WHICH shows the plumbing system.
  • "This is the wing. We will install the new HVAC system there."
    -> This is the wing WHERE we will install the new HVAC system.
  • "There were many suppliers. The ones that offered the best price were chosen."
