Topic: The Architect's Foundation: Basic Structures & Initial Connectors

Consolidation of auxiliary verbs (Do/Does/Did) and transition to logical connectors.

1. Warm-up: The Daily Stand-up

Focus: Do/Does/Did + 3rd Person "S" + Past Tense.

2. Sound Lab: Structural Rhythm (Auxiliaries)

  • "I need the blueprint." → "I DON'T need the blueprint."
  • "She wants a meeting." → "DOES she want a meeting?"
  • "The material arrived." → "DID the material arrive?"

3. Core Drill: The Logic Bricks

  • The HEPA filter is expensive BUT it is necessary.
  • The budget was cut, SO we changed the material.
  • The site is small, THAT'S WHY we use glass.

4. Contextual Injection: Relative Clauses

  • "He is the engineer WHO manages the HVAC system."
  • "That is the material WHICH provides better hygiene."
