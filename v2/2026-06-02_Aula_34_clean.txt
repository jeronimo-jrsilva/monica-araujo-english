[WARM-UP: THE DAILY STAND-UP]
Prompt: "Tell me about your day at the office. What projects did you touch today?"
(Focus: Do/Does/Did + 3rd Person "S" + Past Tense)

[SOUND LAB: STRUCTURAL RHYTHM]
- T: "I need the blueprint." -> S: "I DON'T need the blueprint."
- T: "She wants a meeting." -> S: "DOES she want a meeting?"
- T: "The material arrived." -> S: "DID the material arrive?"
- T: "We have time." -> S: "We DON'T have time."

[CORE DRILL: THE LOGIC BRICKS]
A. Simple Bricks:
1. Client / Like / Modern Style -> S: "The client likes the modern style."
2. We / Not / Finish / The floor plan / Yesterday -> S: "We didn't finish the floor plan yesterday."
3. The Hospital / Need / More space / For the ICU -> S: "The hospital needs more space for the ICU."

B. Connecting Bricks:
1. HEPA Filter / Expensive / But / Necessary -> S: "The HEPA filter is expensive BUT it is necessary."
2. The budget / Cut / So / We / Change / The material -> S: "The budget was cut, SO we changed the material."
3. The site / Be / Small / That's why / We / Use / Glass -> S: "The site is small, THAT'S WHY we use glass."

[CONTEXTUAL INJECTION: THE PROJECT PITCH]
(Use Relative Clauses: Who, Which, That)
- "This is the clinic THAT handles pediatric care."
- "He is the engineer WHO manages the HVAC system."
- "That is the material WHICH provides better hygiene."

[BONUS CHALLENGE: THE NON-STOP MINUTE]
Topic: "Hospital Safety"
Rules: 1 minute talk using: "Because", "So", "Which".
