1. Time Machine: Endurance Drill

Vocabulário:

  • The inspector arrived at the site ten minutes ago. (Vocab: site - local)

  • We have already implemented the new safety measures.

  • I wrote the punch list yesterday afternoon. (Vocab: write/wrote/written)

  • The client hasn’t signed the contract yet. (Vocab: sign - assinar)

  • Have you ever worked with a project like this?

  • Last year, we had a major bottleneck in the ICU wing. (Vocab: bottleneck - gargalo)

  • Since 2024, the hospital has reduced energy waste by 20%. (Vocab: waste - desperdício, since - desde, science - ciência, lead - chumbo, shielding - proteção)

  • I have just talked to the subcontractor.

  • The team finished the foundation before the rain started. (Vocab: before - antes, rain - chuva, start - começar)

  • We were at the site for three hours yesterday.

2. Precision Review: Relative Clauses

(Exercícios de definição de papéis técnicos concluídos com sucesso).

The Context: The government audit starts in 2 hours. There are still issues with the X-Ray shielding and the biohazard signs.

Your Task: Create a 1-minute briefing using:

  1. At least 2 Phrasal Verbs (Carry out, Look into, Iron out, Call off, etc.).

  2. At least 2 Causative Forms (Have something done).

  3. Contrast Connectors (Despite / Although).
