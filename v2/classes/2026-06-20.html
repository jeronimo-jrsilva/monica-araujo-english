Aquecimento, revisão e introdução de vocabulário.

Review (Aula 40)

    • Ex: We ran out of fire-rated glass for the main lobby yesterday.
  • Scaffolding (andaimes):
    • Ex: The storm broke the scaffolding on the north side of the building.
  • Bought vs Brought:
    • Drill: I bought a sample of marble, but the team brought the copper pipes.

New Vocabulary & Context

  • Lead-lining - Revestimento de chumbo:
    • Definition: A protective layer of lead installed in walls or barriers to prevent the transmission of radiation.
    • Ex: You must install lead-lining in the walls of the oncology wing.
  • Shielding - Blindagem contra radiação/impacto:
    • Definition: Materials or structures used to block, absorb, or redirect radiation, sound, or physical impact.
    • Ex: The inspector verified the radiation shielding in the X-Ray room.
  • Snags - Pequenas pendências, falhas ou imperfeições em uma obra:
    • Definition: Minor defects, problems, or unfinished details that appear at the end of a construction project.
    • Ex: We need to iron out the snags before the final audit.
  • Punch list - Lista de pendências e arremates de obra para entrega:
    • Definition: A document listing key tasks, small repairs, and final touch-ups required to complete a project before delivery.
    • Ex: The contractor is working on the final punch list this week.
  • Outlet - Tomada elétrica de parede:
    • Definition: A socket in a wall where electrical devices can be plugged in to receive power.
    • Ex: The project requires extra electrical outlets in the ICU rooms.
