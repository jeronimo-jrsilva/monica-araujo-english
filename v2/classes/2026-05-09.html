Part 1: Technical Vocabulary

Choose the correct word to complete the technical description:

  1. The nurse requested a (fixture / furniture) for the wall to hold the new medical monitor.

  2. During the surgery, we noticed a slight (flicker / glare) in the main light; we need to check the wiring.

  3. To improve patient comfort, we installed a (dimmer / motion sensor) in every private room so they can adjust the light.

  4. The (lighting / lightning) in the corridor is too warm; we should switch to cool LEDs.

  5. We use (emergency / circadian) lighting to help patients maintain a natural sleep cycle in windowless rooms.

Part 2: Adverbs of Intensity

Rewrite the sentences using the adverb in parentheses to change the intensity:

  1. The new LED system is brighter than the old one. (Significantly) Answer: __________________________________________________________________

  2. The emergency lights are visible during the day. (Barely) Answer: __________________________________________________________________

  3. The light in the laboratory is warm. (Slightly) Answer: __________________________________________________________________

  4. The operating room must be bright. (Extremely) Answer: __________________________________________________________________

  5. This hallway is dark compared to the entrance. (Relatively) Answer: __________________________________________________________________

Part 3: Translation Challenge

Translate the following technical requirements to English, using the vocabulary and intensifiers from Class 22:

  1. “Nós precisamos reduzir significativamente o ofuscamento (glare) na recepção.” Answer: __________________________________________________________________

  2. “A luminária (fixture) está piscando (flickering) levemente (slightly).” Answer: __________________________________________________________________

Teacher’s Tip:

  • Warm (/wɔːrm/) vs. Worm (/wɜːrm/) vs. Warn (/wɔːrn/).

  • L.E.D. is pronounced: EL-EE-DEE.

  • Furniture (móveis) vs. Fixture (itens fixos).
