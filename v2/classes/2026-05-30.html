📅 Lesson Info

  • Date: 2026-05-30

Objetivos da Aula

Iniciaremos a trilha de Sentence Linkers, focando em conectores de Adição, Contraste e Causa/Efeito. O objetivo é elevar a sofisticação do discurso executivo da Mônica, permitindo que ela conecte ideias técnicas de forma fluida em reuniões, justificativas de budget e relatórios de conformidade (Incident Reporting).

1. Addition: Expanding Ideas (Saindo do “And”)

Usados para adicionar informações sem repetir “and”. Crucial para listar conquistas ou requisitos:

In addition / Additionally: Além disso (Início de frase). Furthermore / Moreover: Além do mais / Ademais (Mais formal, excelente para e-mails institucionais). As well as: Assim como / Bem como. Not only… but also…: Não apenas… mas também…

Drilling (Healthcare Scenarios):

“The project is on schedule. Furthermore, we stayed 5% under budget.” “We installed the HEPA filters. Moreover, we checked the air exchange rate in the surgical wing.” “The new hospital has a maternity ward as well as an advanced pediatric ICU.” “We need not only the hard hats but also the high-visibility vests for tomorrow’s inspection.”

2. Contrast: Handling Contradictions (Saindo do “But”)

Usados para introduzir imprevistos, exceções ou oposições. Perfeito para gerenciar crises sem perder a compostura:

  • However: No entanto / Porém (O coringa do mundo corporativo).

  • Nonetheless / Nevertheless: No entanto / Mesmo assim (Ainda mais formal, peso jurídico).

  • Although / Even though: Embora / Apesar de (Cria expectativa).

  • Despite / In spite of: Apesar de (CUIDADO: Seguido de substantivo ou verbo com -ING, nunca de uma frase completa).

Drilling (Crisis Management):

  1. “The HVAC system is highly efficient. However, the installation cost is prohibitive.”

  2. “Although the scaffolding was inspected yesterday, we found a minor compliance issue today.”

  3. “Despite the delay in the ductwork delivery, the site remains operational.” (NOT: Despite we had a delay)

  4. “We have a shortage of PPE. Nevertheless, we must enforce the safety protocols.”

3. Cause & Effect: Explaining Decisions (Saindo do “Because”)

Usados para justificar aprovações, atrasos ou mudanças de escopo:

  • Therefore / Thus: Portanto / Sendo assim (Conclusão lógica).

  • Due to / Due to the fact that: Devido a (Muito comum em relatórios técnicos).

  • Consequently / As a result: Consequentemente.

Drilling (Technical Justifications):

  1. “The foundation was compromised. Therefore, we had to halt the concrete pouring.”

  2. “Due to the heavy rain, the external painting was postponed.”

  3. “The supply chain broke down. As a result, we are sourcing materials locally.”

4. Executive Scenario: The Incident & Site Visit Report

Prática de transformação. Mônica deve ler a Versão A (pobre) e reescrevê-la ou ler a Versão B (Executiva).

🔴 Versão A (Basic / Poor): “We went to the site today and we checked the scaffolding. The scaffolding was okay but we saw a worker without a helmet. So we stopped the work because safety is important. And we brought new helmets.”

🟢 Versão B (Executive Gold): “Initially, we conducted a site inspection as well as a scaffolding check. Although the structure was compliant, we identified a worker without the mandatory hard hat. Consequently, operations were temporarily halted due to our strict safety policy. Furthermore, we had new PPE distributed immediately.”

5. Phonetic Lab & Nuances

  • Furthermore: /ˌfɜːrðərˈmɔːr/ (Foco no ‘TH’ vibrado).

  • Moreover: /mɔːrˈoʊvər/.

  • Nevertheless: /ˌnevərðəˈles/ (Ritmo fluido, sem pausas).

  • Although: /ɔːlˈðoʊ/.

  • Con-se-quent-ly: /kɒnsɪkwəntli/ (Atenção à sílaba tônica).
