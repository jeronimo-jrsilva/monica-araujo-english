Class 25: Relative Clauses & Identifying with One/Ones

📅 Lesson Info

  • Date: 2026-05-13
  • Topic: Relative Clauses & Identifying with One/Ones

Warm-up: Passive Voice Review

Review from Class 24 (The “Report” Tone):

  • “We delivered the project yesterday.” -> The project was delivered yesterday.

  • “The technicians follow the safety standards.” -> Safety standards are followed by the technicians.

  • “Mônica chose the sustainable material.” -> The sustainable material was chosen by Mônica.

Contrast: Safety vs. Security

  • Safety: Passive protection (safety standards, safety equipment).

  • Security: Active protection (guards, alarms, active systems).

Grammar 1: Relative Clauses (The Connectors)

Relative Clauses connect ideas and create fluid technical descriptions.

Key Pronouns:

  • WHO (People): For professionals or team members.
    • Ex: “The specialist who calculates the load-bearing capacity…”
  • WHICH (Things): For objects, materials, laws or documents.
    • Ex: “The standard which regulates hospital lighting…”
  • THAT (People/Things): Versatile, used mainly in information essential for the sentence.
    • Ex: “The only material that meets the budget is vinyl.”
  • WHERE (Places): For rooms, sectors or construction sites.
    • Ex: “The area where the vaccines are stored must have backup power.”

Grammar 2: The Power of ONE / ONES

Used to substitute a noun already mentioned, avoiding monotony.

Key Examples:

  • Identifying: “Which layout do you prefer?” -> “I prefer the ones with the open floor plan.”

  • Contrasting: “These fixtures are good, but the ones we used in the previous project were better.”

  • The “Special” One: “The one who leads the BIM coordination is Mônica.”

Practical Drills (Classroom Results)

  1. “We have a contractor. He is responsible for the foundations.” -> The contractor WHO is responsible for the foundations.

  2. “I am looking for the blueprint. It shows the plumbing system.” -> I am looking for the blueprint WHICH shows the plumbing system.

  3. “This is the wing. We will install the new HVAC system there.” -> This is the wing WHERE we will install the new HVAC system.

  4. “There were many suppliers. The ones that offered the best price were chosen.”

Professional Vocabulary & Phonetic Focus

  • The day before yesterday: Anteontem.

  • Plumbing: Encanamento (Attention to the silent ‘b’).

  • Suppliers: Fornecedores.

  • Previous: Anterior.

  • Preview: Previsão.

  • Store / Storage: Armazenar / Armazenamento.

  • Which (/wɪtʃ/) vs Witch (/wɪtʃ/) - Homophones.

  • One (/wʌn/) vs Won (/wʌn/) - Homophones.
