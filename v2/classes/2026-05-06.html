📅 Lesson Info

  • Date: 2026-05-06
  • Topic: Spatial Drills & Hospital Flows (Circulation & Direction)

Conteúdo Efetivo da Aula

  1. Movement Foundations: Domínio das preposições de direção (To, From, Through, Along, Across).

  2. Logística de Fluxos: Aplicação prática em fluxos limpos vs. sujos (Clean/Dirty flows).

  3. Emergency Protocols: Descrição de rotas de fuga e evacuação de pacientes críticos.

  4. Vocabulary Expansion: Termos para logística hospitalar (Stretcher, Waste disposal, Strip).

Vocabulary & Feedback

  • Stretcher: Maca.

  • Sample: Amostra (Ex: “Send the blood sample from the lab to the office”).

  • Waste: Lixo / Descartado (Resíduos).

  • Disposal area: Área de descarte.

  • Signage: Sinalização.

  • Main: Principal (Ex: Main corridor).

  • Reach: Alcançar / Chegar a.

  • Strip: Faixa (Ex: “Follow the blue strip on the floor”).

  • Rooftop: Terraço / Cobertura.

  • Critically ill patients: Pacientes em estado crítico.

Technical Grammar: Prepositions of Movement

1. TO / FROM (Destino & Origem)

  • From the lab to the doctor’s office.

  • From the OR to the recovery room.

2. THROUGH (Através de - Espaço 3D)

  • The stretcher goes through the automatic doors.

  • The air flows through the ducts.

3. ALONG (Ao longo de - Linha/Caminho)

  • Follow the signage along the main corridor.

  • Follow the color strip along the hallway.

4. ACROSS (Através/Cruzando - Superfície 2D)

  • The technicians walk across the lobby to reach the elevators.

  • Cross across the technical area.

5. INTO / OUT OF (Entrada & Saída)

  • The surgeon goes into the sterile zone.

  • The waste is taken out of the dirty utility room.

  • Idiom: “He was kicked out of the conference.”

6. UP / DOWN (Níveis)

  • The service elevator goes up to the rooftop.

  • Patients go down the ramp to the ambulance bay.

Precision Drills: Circulation Dynamics

  1. Waste Flow: “The waste goes out of the room to the disposal area.”

  2. Helipad Access: “We take the service elevator and go up to the 20th (twentieth) floor.”

  3. Emergency: “The team must take the critically ill patients to the emergency elevator and out of the building.”
