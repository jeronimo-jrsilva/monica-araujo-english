Precision in Reporting & The Pre-Audit Challenge

---
1. Precision Review: Relative Clauses
Define these items using WHO / WHICH / THAT:

- A Subcontractor: "A person who..."
- A Blueprint: "A document which..."
- A Bottleneck: "A situation that..."
- The Board: "The group who..."
- A Punch List: "A list which..."
- A Stakeholder: "Anyone who..."

---
2. The Causative Matrix (Delegation)
Structure: HAVE + SOMETHING + DONE

Scenario: The hospital elevator is stuck.
Action: "I'll have it fixed."

Scenario: The floor tiles are cracked.
Action: "I'll have them replaced."

Scenario: The budget was rejected.
Action: "I'll have it revised."

---
3. Challenge: The Pre-Audit Briefing
Goal: 1-minute briefing for your team.

Constraints:
- Use Phrasal Verbs (Carry out, Look into, Iron out).
- Use Causative Forms (Have something done).
- Use Connectors (Despite / Although).

"Despite the delay, we will carry out the final inspection..."

---
4. Vocabulary
- To Brief: To provide essential information.
- To Oversee: To supervise/manage.
- Lead Time: Time between start and finish.
- Punch List: Remaining minor tasks.
- Audit: Auditoria.
